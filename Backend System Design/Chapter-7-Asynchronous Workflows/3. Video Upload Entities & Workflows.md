# Video Upload Entities & Workflows
1. **What are the main entities typically defined in a video processing system design?**
	1. The main entities include: User (the person uploading), Video (source and processed versions), Thumbnails, Audio tracks (separate from video), Metadata (title, resolution, and other properties), and Manifest (determines bit rate and resolution for different clients).
2. **What is the purpose of a manifest in video streaming systems?**
	1. A manifest determines the bit rate and resolution that the client plays at based on calculations performed on the back end. It ensures each device receives the appropriate video quality - for example, sending a lower resolution to phones rather than wasting bandwidth on 4K video that can't be distinguished on small screens.
3. **Why is metadata considered a critical but often forgotten entity in video processing systems?**
	1. Metadata is essential because videos are inherently just a sequence of pictures stitched together with an algorithm. They don't have inherent properties like title or resolution information - this data must be attached separately as metadata to persist throughout the video processing workflow.
4. **In a video upload workflow, why should you not assume that uploads are successful by default?**
	1. Uploads should not be assumed successful because they can fail due to various reasons, especially with large video files. Issues include client errors, slow internet connections that can't handle the upload, or long upload times that may be interrupted. The system needs to handle recovery from failures and notify users when uploads complete successfully.
5. **What is the primary goal of video processing after a successful upload?**
	1. The goal is to take the original uploaded video and break it down into multiple formats and resolutions (such as 4K, HD, mobile-optimized versions) so the video can be displayed appropriately on different clients including mobile devices, TVs, and other platforms. The processed video is typically compressed into streaming-friendly formats.
